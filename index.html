About

Lecturer at Mahidol University

A university instructor-researcher seeking to make English language education a globally-minded learning space. A former PhD student in Education (Applied Linguistics), Department of Education, University of Oxford.

Anuchaya, otherwise known among her English-speaking friends and colleagues as ‘Anya’, is a DPhil (or PhD elsewhere) student within Applied Linguistics research group at the Department of Education, University of Oxford. Prior to embarking on her PhD journey in the UK, Anya was working as a lecturer in an English Language Program at Mahidol University, Thailand, after having previously completed her Master of Education in Teaching English as a Second Language (TESOL) at Boston University in the USA under the Fulbright scholarship. She is currently working on a doctoral thesis entitled “EFL Teachers’ Cognition of English as a Global Language: Ideologies, Identities, and Practices." Her current research interests include Global Englishes (and related fields), Language Teacher Cognition, and Language Teacher Identity.

  • DPhil Education (Applied Linguistics Research Group), University of Oxford, 2021
    EFL Teachers’ Cognition of English as a Global Language: Ideologies, Identities, and Practices
  • Ed.M. Teaching English to Speakers of Other Languages (TESOL), Boston University, 2014
  • B.A. (Hons.) English Language and Literature and Linguistics, Chulalong University, 2012

Research and Teaching Interests

Global Englishes, Language Teacher Cognition, Language Teacher Identity, English as an International Language, English as a Lingua Franca, Learner Attitudes and Identities, English for Specific Purposes, Multiliteracies in language education
